Why Dublin Exit 51?

I-16 × US-441 dual-demand node — capturing Savannah↔Macon travelers AND North↔South US-441 traffic. Land at ~$125K/acre vs. $350–500K/acre on I-75. No Buc-ee's within 45 miles. NEVI-designated Alternative Fuel Corridor — eligible for 80% federal EV subsidy. Laurens County: Georgia's 3rd lowest property tax rate, 100% Freeport exemption. Hurricane evacuation terminus — Exit 51 is where all I-16 contraflow traffic re-enters normal lanes, creating surge demand events.

03 · The Blueprint
Korea proved the model. We bring it to the US.
Korean highway service areas (휴게소) average 8M+ annual visitors. The Anseong / Siheung model has been validated at scale.

Korean Model (Anseong Matgol)

  • Full-service food hall — 12+ vendors
  • Premium restrooms — spa-level finish
  • Regional produce market
  • EV charging hub — 20+ stalls
  • Pet rest zone & playground

Highway Oasis (Dublin)

  • Curated food hall — 6–8 American concepts
  • Premium restrooms — first in class
  • Local Georgia artisan market
  • DC fast charging — Tesla + universal
  • Dog park + kids play zone

Key Adaptation Principles

The concept is translated, not transplanted. Every element passes an American traveler filter:

  • No Korean-branded signage or language in primary zones
  • American food brands and local Georgia producers anchor the food hall
  • Neutral "road trip oasis" identity — premium without ethnic niche positioning
  • Operational model draws from Korean efficiency; customer experience designed for US expectations

16 · Defensibility — Why This is Hard to Copy
The Moat Is Real.
Here's Why.
First-mover advantages compound over time. Each layer below takes 2–5 years for a competitor to replicate.

🏔 Land Scarcity

High-AADT interchange parcels at the right distance from Atlanta are extremely limited. Exit 51 sits in a ~5-mile window where fatigue + fuel logic converge. Once we hold the land, no competitor can replicate the exact position.

⚡ EV Infrastructure Lead

24-stall DC fast charging requires 2–3 year utility permitting + grid upgrade lead time. First installer locks in the charging habit of EV drivers on this corridor for years. Network effects apply.

🍽 Tenant Relationships = Lock-in

Once Chick-fil-A, a regional BBQ anchor, and 4–6 F&B tenants sign 10-year leases, the food hall ecosystem is locked. Competitors can't poach these tenants mid-term, and travelers develop destination habits.

🇰🇷 Korean Operational DNA

The operational efficiency, restroom standards, and customer flow design come from deep familiarity with the Korean model. This isn't publicly documented — it's experiential IP that takes years to develop. Buc-ee's won't copy a Korean휴게소 playbook.

vs. Buc-ee's — Not Actually a Direct Threat

Buc-ee's nearest location (Forsyth, I-75) is 45+ miles away — a different corridor entirely. Their 74,000 SF format requires $50M+ and Texas-scale volumes. Our 18,000–24,000 SF Phase 1 premium format fills the gap they can't serve at Dublin's scale. Once we hold Exit 51, no competitor can replicate the I-16 × US-441 dual-demand position.
